# Chapter 2: Biological Molecules

## Overview

This chapter covers the four major classes of biological molecules:
- Carbohydrates
- Lipids
- Proteins
- Nucleic acids

## Carbohydrates

### Monosaccharides

Simple sugars with the general formula $(CH_2O)_n$.

**Examples:**
- Glucose ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) - hexose sugar
- Fructose - fruit sugar
- Galactose - component of lactose

### Disaccharides

Formed by condensation reactions between two monosaccharides.

| Disaccharide | Components | Linkage |
|--------------|------------|---------|
| Maltose | Glucose + Glucose | α-1,4-glycosidic bond |
| Sucrose | Glucose + Fructose | α-1,2-glycosidic bond |
| Lactose | Glucose + Galactose | β-1,4-glycosidic bond |

### Polysaccharides

**Starch (plants):**
- Amylose - unbranched, helical structure
- Amylopectin - branched structure

**Glycogen (animals):**
- Highly branched structure
- Storage form of glucose in liver and muscle cells

**Cellulose (plants):**
- Structural polysaccharide
- Forms microfibrils in cell walls

## Lipids

### Triglycerides

Structure: Glycerol + 3 fatty acids

```
    CH₂-O-CO-R₁
    |
    CH-O-CO-R₂
    |
    CH₂-O-CO-R₃
```

**Types of fatty acids:**
- **Saturated:** No C=C double bonds (e.g., animal fats)
- **Unsaturated:** Contains C=C double bonds (e.g., vegetable oils)

### Phospholipids

- Amphipathic molecules (hydrophilic head, hydrophobic tails)
- Form bilayers in aqueous environments
- Major component of cell membranes

## Proteins

### Amino Acids

The building blocks of proteins.

**General structure:**
```
        H
        |
    H₂N-C-COOH
        |
        R
```

Where R = variable side chain (20 different amino acids)

### Protein Structure

1. **Primary:** Linear sequence of amino acids (peptide bonds)
2. **Secondary:** α-helices and β-pleated sheets (hydrogen bonds)
3. **Tertiary:** 3D folding (disulfide bonds, ionic bonds, hydrophobic interactions)
4. **Quaternary:** Multiple polypeptide chains

### Enzymes

Biological catalysts that lower activation energy.

$$E_a \text{ (with enzyme)} < E_a \text{ (without enzyme)}$$

**Factors affecting enzyme activity:**
- Temperature
- pH
- Substrate concentration
- Enzyme concentration
- Inhibitors

## Nucleic Acids

### DNA Structure

- Double helix (Watson-Crick model)
- Antiparallel strands (5' → 3' and 3' → 5')
- Complementary base pairing:
  - A = T (2 hydrogen bonds)
  - G ≡ C (3 hydrogen bonds)

### RNA Types

1. **mRNA** - messenger RNA (carries genetic code)
2. **tRNA** - transfer RNA (brings amino acids)
3. **rRNA** - ribosomal RNA (forms ribosomes)

## Key Equations

### Benedict's Test (Reducing Sugars)

$$\text{Cu}^{2+} \text{ (blue)} \rightarrow \text{Cu}_2\text{O} \text{ (brick red precipitate)}$$

### Biuret Test (Proteins)

$$\text{Cu}^{2+} + \text{peptide bonds} \rightarrow \text{violet color}$$
