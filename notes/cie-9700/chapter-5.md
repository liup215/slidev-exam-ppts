# Chapter 5: Cell Division (Mitosis)

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Describe the cell cycle and its phases
2. Explain the stages of mitosis in detail
3. Understand the significance of mitosis
4. Distinguish between mitosis and meiosis
5. Explain the control of cell division

## Key Concepts

### The Cell Cycle

The cell cycle consists of:
- **Interphase:** Cell growth and DNA replication
- **M phase (Mitosis):** Nuclear division
- **Cytokinesis:** Cytoplasmic division

### Interphase

Interphase is divided into three stages:

| Stage | Events | Duration |
|-------|--------|----------|
| G₁ (Gap 1) | Cell growth, protein synthesis, organelle duplication | Variable |
| S (Synthesis) | DNA replication | ~8 hours |
| G₂ (Gap 2) | Cell growth, preparation for mitosis | ~4 hours |

> **Note:** G₀ is a non-dividing state where cells exit the cell cycle.

### Stages of Mitosis

#### Prophase
- Chromatin condenses into visible chromosomes
- Each chromosome consists of two sister chromatids
- Nuclear envelope breaks down
- Centrioles move to opposite poles
- Spindle fibers begin to form

#### Metaphase
- Chromosomes align at the equator (metaphase plate)
- Spindle fibers attach to centromeres
- Chromosomes are maximally condensed

#### Anaphase
- Sister chromatids separate at centromeres
- Chromatids move to opposite poles
- Spindle fibers shorten
- Cell elongates

#### Telophase
- Chromosomes arrive at poles
- Nuclear envelope reforms
- Chromosomes decondense
- Spindle fibers disassemble

### Cytokinesis

#### Animal Cells
- Cleavage furrow forms
- Actin and myosin contract
- Cell pinches in two

#### Plant Cells
- Cell plate forms at equator
- Vesicles fuse to form new cell wall
- Cell wall materials deposited

## Important Formulas

### Chromosome Number Calculations

| Stage | Human Cell (2n = 46) | Description |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| G₁ | 46 chromosomes | Before DNA replication |
| S | 46 → 92 chromatids | DNA replication |
| G₂ | 46 chromosomes (92 chromatids) | After replication |
| Metaphase | 46 chromosomes (92 chromatids) | Aligned at equator |
| Anaphase | 92 chromosomes | Chromatids separate |
| Daughter cells | 46 chromosomes each | After cytokinesis |

### Cell Cycle Duration

$$\text{Mitotic Index} = \frac{\text{Number of cells in mitosis}}{\text{Total number of cells}} \times 100\%$$

> **Note:** High mitotic index indicates rapid cell division (e.g., cancer cells, meristematic tissue).

## Significance of Mitosis

1. **Growth:** Increases organism size
2. **Repair:** Replaces damaged or dead cells
3. **Asexual reproduction:** Produces genetically identical offspring
4. **Maintenance:** Maintains chromosome number

## Comparison: Mitosis vs Meiosis

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Purpose | Growth, repair, asexual reproduction | Sexual reproduction (gamete formation) |
| Number of divisions | 1 | 2 |
| Number of daughter cells | 2 | 4 |
| Chromosome number | Diploid (2n) → Diploid (2n) | Diploid (2n) → Haploid (n) |
| Genetic variation | None (clones) | High (crossing over, independent assortment) |
| Synapsis | No | Yes (prophase I) |
| Crossing over | No | Yes |
| Where it occurs | Somatic cells | Germ cells (testes/ovaries) |

### Detailed Comparison Table

| Stage | Mitosis | Meiosis I | Meiosis II |
|-------|---------|-----------|------------|
| Prophase | Chromosomes condense | Homologous pairs synapse | Similar to mitosis |
| Metaphase | Single file at equator | Pairs at equator | Single file at equator |
| Anaphase | Sister chromatids separate | Homologous chromosomes separate | Sister chromatids separate |
| Result | 2 identical cells | 2 haploid cells | 4 haploid cells |

## Control of Cell Division

### Checkpoints

The cell cycle is regulated at three main checkpoints:

| Checkpoint | Location | Function |
|------------|----------|----------|
| G₁/S | End of G₁ | Checks cell size, nutrients, DNA damage |
| G₂/M | End of G₂ | Checks DNA replication completion |
| Spindle | Metaphase | Checks chromosome attachment to spindle |

### Cyclins and CDKs

- **Cyclins:** Regulatory proteins whose levels fluctuate
- **CDKs (Cyclin-Dependent Kinases):** Enzymes activated by cyclins
- Together form **MPF (Maturation Promoting Factor)**

$$\text{Cyclin} + \text{CDK} \rightarrow \text{Active MPF} \rightarrow \text{Cell enters mitosis}$$

### Tumor Suppressor Genes

- **p53:** "Guardian of the genome"
- Stops cell cycle if DNA is damaged
- Mutations lead to uncontrolled cell division (cancer)
