# Chapter 3: Enzymes

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain the nature of enzymes as biological catalysts
2. Describe the mechanism of enzyme action (lock and key, induced fit)
3. Understand the factors affecting enzyme activity
4. Distinguish between competitive and non-competitive inhibition
5. Explain the concept of enzyme specificity

## Key Concepts

### Nature of Enzymes

Enzymes are globular proteins that act as biological catalysts:
- **Catalysts:** Speed up chemical reactions without being consumed
- **Specificity:** Each enzyme catalyzes only one type of reaction
- **Efficiency:** Can increase reaction rates by 10⁶ to 10¹² times

### Mechanism of Enzyme Action

#### Lock and Key Hypothesis
- Enzyme has a fixed active site (lock)
- Substrate fits exactly into the active site (key)
- Forms enzyme-substrate (ES) complex

#### Induced Fit Hypothesis (Modern View)
- Active site is flexible
- Substrate binding causes conformational change
- Better explains enzyme specificity

### Enzyme-Substrate Complex

$$E + S \rightleftharpoons ES \rightarrow E + P$$

Where:
- E = Enzyme
- S = Substrate
- ES = Enzyme-Substrate complex
- P = Product

## Important Formulas

### Michaelis-Menten Equation

$$v = \frac{V_{max}[S]}{K_m + [S]}$$

Where:
- $v$ = reaction rate
- $V_{max}$ = maximum reaction rate
- $[S]$ = substrate concentration
- $K_m$ = Michaelis constant (substrate concentration at half $V_{max}$)

### Activation Energy

$$E_a \text{ (with enzyme)} \ll E_a \text{ (without enzyme)}$$

> **Note:** Enzymes lower activation energy but do NOT change the overall free energy change (ΔG) of the reaction.

## Factors Affecting Enzyme Activity

### Temperature

| Temperature Effect | Explanation |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Low temperature | Reduced kinetic energy, fewer collisions |
| Optimum temperature | Maximum enzyme activity |
| High temperature | Denaturation of enzyme (breaks H bonds) |

**Typical optimum temperatures:**
- Human enzymes: 37°C (body temperature)
- Thermophilic bacteria: 70-80°C

### pH

- Each enzyme has an optimum pH
- pH affects ionization of amino acid side chains
- Extreme pH causes denaturation

| Enzyme | Optimum pH | Location |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Pepsin | 1.5-2.0 | Stomach |
| Trypsin | 8.0 | Small intestine |
| Amylase | 6.7-7.0 | Mouth/saliva |
| Catalase | 7.0 | Liver cells |

### Substrate Concentration

- At low [S]: Rate proportional to [S]
- At high [S]: Rate plateaus (enzyme saturated)
- All active sites occupied at $V_{max}$

### Enzyme Concentration

- Rate directly proportional to enzyme concentration (when substrate is in excess)

## Types of Inhibition

### Competitive Inhibition

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Mechanism | Inhibitor competes with substrate for active site |
| Structure | Inhibitor resembles substrate |
| Effect on $V_{max}$ | Unchanged |
| Effect on $K_m$ | Increased |
| Reversibility | Reversible (can be overcome by increasing [S]) |

**Example:** Malonate inhibits succinate dehydrogenase

### Non-Competitive Inhibition

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Mechanism | Inhibitor binds to allosteric site (not active site) |
| Structure | Different from substrate |
| Effect on $V_{max}$ | Decreased |
| Effect on $K_m$ | Unchanged |
| Reversibility | May be reversible or irreversible |

**Example:** Cyanide inhibits cytochrome oxidase

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Competitive | Non-Competitive |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Binding site | Active site | Allosteric site |
| Resembles substrate | Yes | No |
| $V_{max}$ | Same | Lower |
| $K_m$ | Higher | Same |
| Overcome by excess substrate | Yes | No |

## Cofactors and Coenzymes

### Cofactors
- Non-protein components required for enzyme activity
- Types: Metal ions (Fe²⁺, Mg²⁺, Zn²⁺, Cu²⁺)

### Coenzymes
- Organic cofactors
- Often derived from vitamins
- Examples: NAD⁺, FAD, Coenzyme A

## Exam Tips

1. **Remember:** Enzymes are proteins (except ribozymes/RNA enzymes)
2. **Key point:** Enzymes lower activation energy but don't change ΔG
3. **Graph interpretation:** Be able to sketch and interpret enzyme activity graphs
4. **Inhibition:** Know the differences between competitive and non-competitive inhibition
5. **Denaturation:** Understand that high temperature and extreme pH cause permanent loss of structure

## Practice Questions

1. Explain why increasing substrate concentration increases reaction rate only up to a certain point.

2. Compare and contrast competitive and non-competitive inhibition.

3. Sketch a graph showing the effect of temperature on enzyme activity and explain the shape.

4. Calculate the reaction rate when $V_{max}$ = 100 μmol/min, $K_m$ = 5 mM, and [S] = 5 mM.
