# Chapter 1: Cell Structure

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Describe the structure of eukaryotic cells
2. Distinguish between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
3. Explain the functions of cellular organelles
4. Understand the role of the cytoskeleton

## Key Concepts

### Cell Theory

The cell theory states that:
- All living organisms are composed of cells
- The cell is the basic unit of life
- All cells come from pre-existing cells

### Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

| Feature | Prokaryotic | Eukaryotic |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Nucleus | No membrane-bound nucleus | Has membrane-bound nucleus |
| DNA | Circular, naked | Linear, associated with histones |
| Organelles | No membrane-bound organelles | Has membrane-bound organelles |
| Size | 0.5-5 μm | 10-100 μm |
| Examples | Bacteria, Archaea | Plants, animals, fungi, protists |

### Eukaryotic Cell Structure

#### Nucleus
The control center of the cell containing genetic material (DNA).

**Functions:**
- Stores genetic information
- Controls gene expression
- Site of DNA replication

#### Mitochondria
The "powerhouse" of the cell.

$$ATP = ADP + P_i + \text{energy}$$

**Key features:**
- Double membrane structure
- Contains its own DNA
- Site of cellular respiration

#### Chloroplasts (Plant cells only)
Site of photosynthesis.

$$6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{light energy} \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$$

## Important Formulas

### Magnification Formula

$$\text{Magnification} = \frac{\text{Image size}}{\text{Actual size}}$$

### Surface Area to Volume Ratio

$$\text{SA:V} = \frac{\text{Surface Area}}{\text{Volume}}$$

> **Note:** As cells increase in size, the SA:V ratio decreases, limiting the rate of diffusion.

## Exam Tips

1. Always label diagrams clearly
2. Remember the differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
3. Understand the relationship between structure and function
4. Be able to calculate magnification and actual size
