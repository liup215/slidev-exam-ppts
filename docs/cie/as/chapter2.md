## Key Concepts

### Carbohydrates

**Monosaccharides** (single sugars):
- Glucose - main energy source
- Fructose - fruit sugar
- Galactose - milk sugar

**Disaccharides** (two sugars):
- Maltose = glucose + glucose
- Sucrose = glucose + fructose
- Lactose = glucose + galactose

**Polysaccharides**:
- Starch - energy storage in plants
- Glycogen - energy storage in animals
- Cellulose - structural in plants

### Lipids

**Triglycerides:**
- Glycerol + 3 fatty acids
- Energy storage
- Insulation
- Protection

**Phospholipids:**
- Glycerol + 2 fatty acids + phosphate
- Cell membrane structure

### Proteins

**Structure levels:**
1. Primary - amino acid sequence
2. Secondary - α-helix or β-pleated sheet
3. Tertiary - 3D folding
4. Quaternary - multiple polypeptide chains

### Enzymes

**Key properties:**
- Biological catalysts
- Specific active site
- Lower activation energy
- Affected by temperature and pH

**Factors affecting enzyme activity:**
- Temperature
- pH
- Substrate concentration
- Enzyme concentration

## Biological Molecule Tests

| Molecule | Test | Positive Result |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| Reducing sugars | Benedict's test | Brick red precipitate |
| Starch | Iodine test | Blue-black color |
| Proteins | Biuret test | Purple/violet color |
| Lipids | Emulsion test | White emulsion |

---

## Study Tips

1. **Learn the bonds** - glycosidic, ester, peptide bonds
2. **Practice tests** - know the reagents and results
3. **Draw structures** - glucose, amino acids, triglycerides
4. **Understand enzymes** - lock and key vs induced fit
