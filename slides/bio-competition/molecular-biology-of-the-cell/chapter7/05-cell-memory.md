# Mechanisms That Reinforce Cell Memory in Plants and Animals

## 植物和动物中强化细胞记忆的机制

---

## DNA Methylation and Inheritance

### Vertebrate DNA Methylation
- **5-methylcytosine**: Methylation occurs primarily at CG sequences (CpG dinucleotides)
- **Maintenance methyltransferase**: Recognizes hemi-methylated DNA after replication and methylates daughter strand
- **Inheritance mechanism**: Parent strand methylation pattern serves as template for daughter strand

### Developmental Dynamics
- **Genome-wide demethylation**: After fertilization, most methyl groups are lost
- **De novo methylation**: Later in development, ~70% of CG sequences become methylated
- **Gene repression**: Dense methylation helps repress transcription through:
  - Direct interference with protein binding
  - Recruitment of methyl-binding proteins that associate with histone-modifying enzymes

> **Key Point**: DNA methylation provides stable, heritable gene repression that is much less "leaky" than bacterial systems

## CG Islands and Gene Regulation

### CG Island Characteristics
- **Evolutionary preservation**: CG sequences normally mutate to TG over time due to deamination of 5-methylcytosine
- **Island formation**: CG islands (~1000 nucleotide pairs) remain unmethylated in germ line, preserving CG density
- **Promoter association**: ~60% of human protein-coding genes have promoters in CG islands, including housekeeping genes

### Unmethylated CG Island Properties
- **Chromatin accessibility**: Proteins binding unmethylated CG sequences recruit histone-modifying enzymes that decompress chromatin
- **Promoter-friendly**: RNA polymerase often bound even when gene not actively transcribed
- **Competition advantage**: Polymerase binding favored over nucleosome assembly

## Genomic Imprinting

### Parent-of-Origin Effects
- **Monoallelic expression**: Only one parental copy expressed, other silenced by methylation
- **Human examples**: ~300 imprinted genes; mutations can be "unmasked" if functional copy is silenced
- **Angelman syndrome**: Results from gene deletion on one chromosome + imprinting silencing on other

### Igf2 Imprinting Mechanism
- **Paternal expression**: Only paternal Igf2 copy transcribed
- **Insulator control**: 
  - **Maternal chromosome**: CTCF binds insulator, blocks enhancer access to Igf2
  - **Paternal chromosome**: Insulator methylated, CTCF cannot bind, enhancer activates Igf2

### Kcnq1 Imprinting via lncRNA
- **Paternal silencing**: lncRNA transcribed only from paternal allele
- **Chromatin modification**: lncRNA recruits histone-modifying and DNA-methylating enzymes
- **Maternal protection**: Methylation blocks lncRNA synthesis, allowing Kcnq1 expression

> **Evolutionary mystery**: Imprinting may reflect evolutionary struggle between male/female interests in offspring size

## X-Chromosome Inactivation

### Dosage Compensation
- **Problem**: Females have 2X chromosomes vs male 1X, but need equal gene expression
- **Solution**: Random inactivation of one X chromosome in female somatic cells
- **Mosaic pattern**: Female is mosaic of clones with either maternal or paternal X active

### Xist RNA Mechanism
- **Long noncoding RNA**: ~20,000 nucleotide transcript coats the chromosome that produces it
- **Protein recruitment**: Xist binds proteins including DNA methylases, histone-modifying enzymes, structural components
- **Chromosome condensation**: Extensive methylation and compaction creates transcriptionally resistant heterochromatin

### Escape Genes
- **15-20% escape**: Some genes remain active on inactive X chromosome
- **Loop structures**: Escapee genes form chromatin loops extending from compact chromosome interior
- **CTCF involvement**: Active genes occur in CTCF-rich regions, unlike silenced interior

> **Species differences**: Placental mammals use random inactivation; marsupials silence paternal X; flies upregulate male X; worms downregulate both X's in hermaphrodites

## Epigenetic Inheritance Mechanisms

### Four Distinct Mechanisms
**Cis-acting mechanisms** (affect single chromosome):
1. **DNA methylation**: Maintenance methylase propagates methylation patterns
2. **Histone modification**: "Reader-writer" proteins replicate chromatin states

**Trans-acting mechanisms** (affect both chromosomes):
3. **Positive feedback loops**: Transcription regulators activate their own expression
4. **Protein aggregation**: Prion-like conformational changes transmit gene expression states

### Monoallelic Expression Beyond X-inactivation
- **Additional genes**: 1000-2000 human genes show random monoallelic expression
- **Somatic mosaicism**: Different cell clones express different gene copies
- **Late development**: Choice often made relatively late, creating tissue mosaics

> **Definition**: Epigenetic inheritance = heritable phenotypic changes without DNA sequence alteration
