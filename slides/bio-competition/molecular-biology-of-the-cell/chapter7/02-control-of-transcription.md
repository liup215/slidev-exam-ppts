## Transcription Regulators and cis-Regulatory Sequences

- **Transcription regulators**: ~10% of protein-coding genes encode these proteins
- **cis-regulatory sequences**: Short DNA sequences (5-12 nucleotide pairs) recognized by transcription regulators
- **Gene control region**: Contains promoter + all cis-regulatory sequences that control transcription rate

> **Key Concept**: The positions, identity, and arrangement of cis-regulatory sequences determine when and where each gene is transcribed

## How Proteins Read DNA Sequence

### DNA Double Helix Structure
- **Major groove**: Wider, displays more molecular features - primary site for protein recognition
- **Minor groove**: Narrower, fewer distinguishing features

### Recognition Mechanisms
- Hydrogen-bond donors and acceptors
- Hydrophobic patches (methyl groups)
- Each base pair projects unique pattern in major groove

## Common Structural Motifs in Transcription Regulators

### Helix-Turn-Helix
- Two α-helices connected by short turn
- C-terminal helix = recognition helix fits into major groove
- Found in bacteria, eukaryotes, and archaea

### Zinc Finger
- Zinc atom holds α-helix and β-sheet together
- Often found in clusters along DNA major groove
- Creates continuous stretch of α-helices

### Leucine Zipper
- Two α-helices form coiled-coil dimerization interface
- Grips DNA like clothespin on clothesline

### Homeodomain
- Three α-helices packed tightly
- Helix 3 = recognition helix contacts major groove
- Flexible arm contacts minor groove

## Dimerization and Cooperative Binding

### Why Dimerize?
- **Monomer**: Recognizes 4-8 nucleotide pairs
- **Dimer**: Recognizes 8-12+ nucleotide pairs
- **Result**: Greatly increased specificity and affinity

### Cooperative Binding
- **Noncooperative**: Stable dimers bind independently
- **Cooperative**: Weak monomers form dimers only on DNA
- **S-shaped binding curve**: All-or-none binding behavior

### Nucleosome Effects
- Nucleosomes generally inhibit transcription regulator binding
- **"Breathing"**: Transient DNA exposure at nucleosome ends
- **Pioneer factors**: Can bind nucleosomal DNA and initiate chromatin opening
- **Cooperative invasion**: One regulator facilitates binding of others

> **Dynamic Nature**: Transcription regulators rapidly bind and dissociate from DNA in live cells
