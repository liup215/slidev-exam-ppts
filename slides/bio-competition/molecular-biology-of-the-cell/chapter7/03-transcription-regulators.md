# Transcription Regulators Switch Genes On and Off

## 转录调控因子开启和关闭基因

---

## Bacterial Gene Regulation: Simpler Systems

### The Tryptophan Repressor
- **Operon structure**: 5 genes transcribed as single mRNA for tryptophan biosynthesis
- **Repression mechanism**: 
  - Tryptophan binds repressor → conformational change → repressor binds operator
  - Blocks RNA polymerase access to promoter
- **Allosteric regulation**: Repressor always present, responds rapidly to tryptophan levels

### Activators vs Repressors
- **Repressors**: Block transcription (e.g., tryptophan repressor)
- **Activators**: Help RNA polymerase initiate transcription (e.g., CAP protein)
- **CAP activation**: Requires cAMP binding when glucose is absent

---

## The Lac Operon: Integrated Control

### Dual Regulation Logic
- **Lac repressor**: Shuts off operon when lactose absent
- **CAP activator**: Turns on operon when glucose absent
- **Logic gate**: Operon ON only when **lactose present AND glucose absent**

### DNA Looping in Bacteria
- **Exception to proximity rule**: Some cis-regulatory sequences located thousands of nucleotides away
- **Looping mechanism**: DNA loops out to allow distant regulator to contact RNA polymerase
- **Tether effect**: Dramatically increases collision probability between regulator and polymerase

## Eukaryotic Gene Regulation: Complex Systems

### Gene Control Regions
- **Extended control regions**: Regulatory sequences spread over tens of thousands of nucleotide pairs
- **Spacer DNA**: Non-recognized sequences between regulatory elements
- **DNA looping**: Essential for bringing distant regulators to promoter

### Transcription Regulator Complexes
- **Cooperative assembly**: Multiple regulators bind together on DNA
- **Coactivators/co-repressors**: Multisubunit complexes recruited by DNA-bound regulators
- **Shared components**: Individual regulators can participate in both activating and repressing complexes

> **Key Difference**: Bacterial regulators contact RNA polymerase directly; eukaryotic regulators work through intermediaries (Mediator, chromatin modifiers)

---

## Mechanisms of Eukaryotic Activation

### Mediator Complex
- **Bridge function**: Connects DNA-bound activators to RNA polymerase II and general transcription factors
- **Size**: ~30 subunits, similar size to RNA polymerase itself

### Chromatin Modification
- **Nucleosome barrier**: General transcription machinery cannot assemble on nucleosomal DNA
- **Activation strategies**:
  - Histone modifications (acetylation, phosphorylation)
  - Nucleosome remodeling and sliding
  - Histone removal and replacement
  - Recruitment of bromodomain-containing proteins

### Paused RNA Polymerase
- **Common in humans**: Significant fraction of inactive genes have polymerase paused after ~50 nucleotides
- **Rapid response**: Bypasses slow assembly steps for quick gene activation
- **Release mechanisms**: Chromatin remodeling, elongation factor loading, coactivator signaling

---

## Transcriptional Synergy and Condensates

### Transcriptional Synergy
- **Multiplicative effect**: Multiple activators working together produce much higher transcription than sum of individual effects
- **Energy barrier reduction**: Each factor lowers free-energy barrier, combined effect is exponential

### Biomolecular Condensates
- **Phase separation**: Hundreds of proteins form localized condensates at complex gene control regions
- **Efficiency**: Proteins retained nearby when they dissociate, enabling rapid re-binding
- **Dynamic nature**: Continuous exchange of components maintains active transcription state

---

## Eukaryotic Repression Mechanisms

### Six Major Strategies
1. **Competitive DNA binding**: Repressor outcompetes activator for same site
2. **Masking activation surface**: Repressor blocks activator's functional domain
3. **Direct interference**: Repressor poisons general transcription factor assembly
4. **Chromatin remodeling**: Restores nucleosomal state to default repressed form
5. **Histone deacetylation**: Removes acetylation marks needed for transcription
6. **Heterochromatin formation**: Creates self-propagating, highly condensed chromatin state

### Insulator Elements
- **Function**: Prevent inappropriate cross-talk between adjacent gene control regions
- **Mechanism**: Form chromatin loops that isolate regulatory domains
- **CTCF protein**: Major insulator-binding protein in mammals
