# Regulation of Gene Expression by Noncoding RNAs

## 非编码RNA对基因表达的调控

---

## RNA Interference Overview

### Core Mechanism
- **Guide RNAs**: Short single-stranded RNAs (20-30 nucleotides) locate targets through complementary base-pairing
- **Three main classes**: microRNAs (miRNAs), small interfering RNAs (siRNAs), piwi-interacting RNAs (piRNAs)
- **Multiple outcomes**:
  - mRNA cleavage and degradation
  - Translational repression
  - Heterochromatin formation on transcribed DNA

### Argonaute Proteins
- **Central effector**: Holds guide RNA in extended conformation for optimal target binding
- **Slicer activity**: Catalytic site cleaves target RNA when base-pairing is extensive
- **Versatility**: Some Argonaute proteins lack catalytic activity but still mediate translational repression

## microRNAs (miRNAs)

### Biogenesis and Processing
- **Genomic origin**: >1000 different miRNAs from human genome, regulate ~50% of protein-coding genes
- **Processing pathway**: Primary transcript → nuclear cropping → cytoplasmic dicing by Dicer → mature miRNA
- **RISC assembly**: miRNA loaded into RNA-induced silencing complex with Argonaute protein

### Target Recognition and Regulation
- **Seed region**: 7-nucleotide match near 5' end of miRNA (common in animals)
- **Extensive pairing**: Complete complementarity leads to mRNA cleavage (common in plants)
- **Combinatorial control**: Single miRNA can regulate hundreds of mRNAs; multiple miRNAs can cooperatively regulate single mRNA

### Functional Outcomes
- **Translational repression**: Recruitment of deadenylases and translation blockers
- **mRNA degradation**: Eventual destruction after translational repression
- **P-body localization**: Repressed mRNAs often sequestered in P-bodies for storage or degradation

## siRNAs and Cellular Defense

### Viral and Transposon Defense
- **Double-stranded RNA trigger**: Viruses and transposons produce dsRNA during replication
- **Dicer processing**: dsRNA cleaved into ~23 bp siRNA duplexes
- **Amplification mechanism**: RNA-dependent RNA polymerases use siRNAs as primers to produce more dsRNA

### Systemic Immunity
- **Cell-to-cell spread**: RNA fragments move between cells (especially important in plants with plasmodesmata)
- **Evolutionary relationship**: siRNAs likely represent ancient defense system; miRNAs evolved as regulatory refinement
- **Mammalian adaptation**: Protein-based immune system largely replaced RNAi for viral defense

## RNAi-Directed Heterochromatin Formation

### RITS Complex
- **RNA-induced transcriptional silencing**: siRNA guides complex to nascent transcripts
- **Chromatin modification**: Recruitment of histone methyltransferases (H3K9me3) and DNA methylases
- **Constitutive heterochromatin**: Creates transcriptionally resistant chromatin state

### Biological Functions
- **Transposon control**: Maintains transposable elements in silenced state
- **Centromere maintenance**: Centromeric DNA transcribed bidirectionally → dsRNA formation → heterochromatin formation
- **Positive feedback**: Continuous siRNA production ensures long-term silencing even after initial trigger disappears

---

## piRNAs and Germline Protection

### Unique Characteristics
- **Germline-specific**: Protect gametes from transposon activity during vulnerable periods
- **piRNA clusters**: Genomic regions rich in transposon fragments serve as "hit list" for surveillance
- **Processing differences**: Dicer-independent processing; 2'-O-methylation at 3' end

### Amplification and Adaptation
- **Ping-pong cycle**: Cleavage of transposon mRNA generates new piRNAs from adjacent sequences
- **Adaptive response**: Incorporates sequence information from active transposons not in original clusters
- **Self vs non-self discrimination**: Mechanisms to spare legitimate cellular mRNAs from piRNA attack

### Evolutionary Arms Race
- **Millions of piRNAs**: Mammalian genomes encode vast numbers, many targeting historical transposon invasions
- **Rapid evolution**: piRNA system constantly adapts to new transposon threats

## CRISPR: Bacterial Adaptive Immunity

### Three-Step Process
1. **Integration**: Viral DNA fragments inserted into CRISPR locus as "vaccination record"
2. **crRNA production**: CRISPR locus transcribed and processed into guide RNAs
3. **Target destruction**: crRNA-Cas complexes seek and destroy matching viral sequences

### Key Features
- **Temporal record**: Newest viral sequences integrated at 5' end of CRISPR locus
- **PAM requirement**: Protospacer adjacent motifs prevent self-targeting of CRISPR locus
- **Diverse systems**: Multiple CRISPR-Cas variants across bacterial and archaeal species

### Anti-CRISPR Countermeasures
- **Viral evasion**: Anti-CRISPR proteins that inhibit Cas function
- **Protective coatings**: Special structures that shield viral DNA during replication

> **Biotechnological impact**: CRISPR systems adapted for genome editing in eukaryotes (covered in Chapter 8)

## Long Noncoding RNAs (lncRNAs)

### Definition and Diversity
- **Arbitrary cutoff**: >200 nucleotides, no protein coding potential
- **Sheer numbers**: ~5000 estimated in human genome
- **Transcription noise challenge**: Difficult to distinguish functional lncRNAs from background transcription

### Three Key Properties
1. **Scaffold function**: Hold multiple proteins together to coordinate activities (e.g., telomerase RNA, Xist)
2. **Guide function**: Base-pair with specific DNA/RNA targets to localize bound proteins
3. **Condensate organization**: Drive formation of biomolecular condensates through multivalent interactions

### Functional Mechanisms
- **Cis vs trans action**: 
  - **Cis**: Affect only chromosome of origin (e.g., Xist, some imprinting RNAs)
  - **Trans**: Diffuse to affect other chromosomes or cellular locations
- **Direct base-pairing**: Antisense lncRNAs block translation or alter splicing patterns
- **Molecular sponges**: Sequester miRNAs or RNA-binding proteins to modulate their activity

### Notable Examples
- **Xist RNA**: Coats inactive X chromosome, recruits silencing machinery
- **Imprinting lncRNAs**: Silence paternal Kcnq1 allele through chromatin modification
- **Telomerase RNA**: Template for telomere synthesis and scaffold for telomerase complex

> **Functional mystery**: Majority of lncRNAs have unknown functions; field still in early discovery phase

## Summary: RNA's Expanding Roles

### Beyond the Central Dogma
- **Information carriers**: mRNA (traditional role)
- **Catalytic molecules**: rRNA, ribozymes
- **Regulatory guides**: miRNAs, siRNAs, piRNAs, crRNAs
- **Structural scaffolds**: lncRNAs, telomerase RNA
- **Defense systems**: RNAi, CRISPR

### Evolutionary Perspectives
- **Ancient origins**: RNA world hypothesis supported by RNA's diverse capabilities
- **Layered complexity**: New RNA functions built upon existing molecular machinery
- **Host-parasite arms races**: Much RNA biology driven by evolutionary conflicts with viruses and transposons

> **Key insight**: RNA molecules are not just passive messengers but active participants in nearly every aspect of gene regulation and genome defense
